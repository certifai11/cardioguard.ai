About This Tool

Purpose

The Heart Disease Risk Assessment Tool is designed to provide an initial screening for heart disease risk factors. It uses a machine learning model trained on clinical data to estimate the probability of heart disease based on various patient parameters.

How It Works

Our tool uses a supervised machine learning model that has been trained on the Cleveland Heart Disease dataset from the UCI Machine Learning Repository. The model analyzes 13 clinical parameters to estimate the probability of coronary heart disease.

Important: This tool is intended for educational and informational purposes only. It is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always seek the advice of your physician or other qualified health provider with any questions you may have regarding a medical condition.

Key Features Used in the Model

  • Age and Sex: Basic demographic information that influences heart disease risk
  • Chest Pain Type: The type of chest pain experienced, categorized into four types
  • Blood Pressure and Cholesterol: Key cardiovascular health metrics
  • ECG Results: Electrical activity of the heart at rest
  • Maximum Heart Rate: Highest heart rate achieved during exercise
  • Exercise-Induced Angina: Chest pain caused by physical exertion
  • ST Depression: Measure of abnormality in ECG during exercise
  • Number of Major Vessels: Number of major blood vessels colored by fluoroscopy
  • Thalassemia: Blood disorder that can affect heart function

Model Performance

Our model achieves approximately 86% accuracy on test data, with a balanced precision and recall. The model was validated using 10-fold cross-validation to ensure robustness.

Limitations

While this tool can provide a risk assessment based on the provided information, it has several limitations:

  • It is not a diagnostic tool and cannot definitively determine whether you have heart disease
  • It does not consider all possible risk factors for heart disease
  • The model is trained on historical data which may not represent all populations equally
  • Individual variations and unique medical histories may not be adequately captured

Understanding Your Results

Risk Levels

High Risk (≥70%)

A high-risk result indicates a significant probability of coronary heart disease. It is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional as soon as possible for a comprehensive evaluation.

Recommended Actions:

  • Schedule an appointment with a cardiologist
  • Follow up with additional diagnostic tests as recommended by your doctor
  • Review and modify lifestyle factors that may be contributing to risk
Moderate Risk (50-69%)

A moderate-risk result suggests that there are some concerning factors that may increase your risk of heart disease. It is advisable to discuss these results with your healthcare provider.

Recommended Actions:

  • Schedule a check-up with your primary care physician
  • Consider lifestyle modifications to reduce risk factors
  • Monitor your blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar regularly
Low Risk (<50%)

A low-risk result suggests that your current risk factors do not strongly indicate heart disease. However, it's still important to maintain heart-healthy habits and receive regular check-ups.

Recommended Actions:

  • Continue regular health check-ups
  • Maintain a heart-healthy lifestyle (balanced diet, regular exercise, avoid smoking)
  • Stay informed about heart disease prevention
Note: Risk percentages are estimates based on available data and are not definitive diagnoses. Always consult with healthcare professionals for proper medical advice.

Technical Information

Technologies Used

  • Backend: Flask (Python web framework)
  • Machine Learning: Scikit-learn, NumPy, Pandas
  • Frontend: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Bootstrap
  • Model: Gradient Boosting Classifier

Data Processing

The data processing pipeline includes:

  • Feature scaling to normalize numerical variables
  • Missing value imputation using median values
  • Categorical feature encoding
  • Feature selection based on importance ranking

Model Training

The model was trained using a Gradient Boosting Classifier with hyperparameters optimized through grid search. Cross-validation was performed to ensure model stability and generalizability.
